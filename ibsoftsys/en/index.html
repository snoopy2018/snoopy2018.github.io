SMARTPHONE APP
SMARTPHONE APP

The idea for this app emerged at Laurelwood Elementary School in Santa Clara, California. As a volunteer, I played the card game “SET” with second-graders during the breaks in math class. The game trains visual perception and logical thinking. I kept imagining how a smartphone could take over my role as a referee.

Based on this vision, I developed the smartphone app AI Card Player. The app recognizes the colors and symbols of the playing cards, calculates all permitted combinations according to the rules of the game and presents the solution to the puzzle in a clear and understandable way.

Most of the development has been finding a reliable way to recognize the colors and symbols of the playing cards and translating that model into effective software. Interestingly, the visual perception of playing cards and the learning of the symbols is a relatively easy task for us humans, but they do not represent a trivial problem for a computer system. In contrast, the replication of logical thinking seems to be child's play for a computer system. The implementation of the rules of the game only accounted for a tiny fraction of the development effort. You can find more information about the app at the webpage AI Card Player.

WEB PAGE FILTER
WEB PAGE FILTER

A customer from Berlin wanted software that would search the generated websites of an online vehicle market according to selected criteria and filter out contact addresses that were of interest to him from the flood of information.

A challenge in this project was the high number of requests to the web server of the online portal (more than 50,000) and the associated time delays or even interruptions by the web server.

The solution to the problem was to break queries into smaller units and save the intermediate results in a standard format. As a result, the data could ultimately be merged into the desired dealer lists regardless of the Internet connection. The generated list can now be easily imported from a standard program, for example with Microsoft Excel or Apple Numbers, and displayed clearly as a table.

This solution made the software system not only more robust and scalable, but also faster.

SIMULATION
SIMULATION

General Motors (GM) in the USA was looking for a simulation model for distributed electronic control units (ECUs) in their vehicles. The overriding goal was to develop optimal system architectures for future vehicle models.

Together with a scientist from the GM Advanced Technology Office in Palo Alto, I developed a model in the MLDesigner simulation program that can simulate the distributed ECUs. The heart of the model was a search algorithm that analyzed all possible communication channels and thus enabled a quantitative evaluation of different system architectures. Individually developed evaluation functions also allowed a simple analysis of the simulation results and rounded off the developed software package.

The simulation model, which is based on statistical methods, was accepted by the engineers and successfully used in the development department at GM.

EMBEDDED SYSTEMS
EMBEDDED SYSTEMS

At the BMW Group in Munich, I worked in interdisciplinary engineering teams on the development of embedded and safety-critical systems (for example, engine controls or steering systems).

My task was the development of real-time critical software systems that were used with different hardware platforms in different vehicle models. My area of work included the creation of the first prototypes in the laboratory, the subsequent integration into the vehicle with system tests under normal and extreme climatic conditions as well as the support of the software systems after the production of the vehicles.

Ever more complex systems in the vehicle (for example, Drive-by-Wire) required the use of new development methods and tools. In order to master this complexity for future vehicle models, I developed and evaluated new software development approaches for such systems as part of my doctorate in a research team at the BMW Technology Office in Palo Alto, California.
