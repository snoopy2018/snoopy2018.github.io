Introducing the Artificial Intelligent Card Player

Practice your rational reasoning and take it to the next level. AI Card Player is a computer vision app running on iPhone devices. It helps you to learn and master SET, a card game of visual perception. The app analyses images captured by camera or from file, identifies color, shape, shading, and number of cards, visualizes game rules, and provides hints for finding a set. Turn your phone into a companion of an fascinating and fun card game.

Do you need a hint? The app provides hints whenever you need little help to find a set. The clue is based on the sets in the scene, cards you've selected, sets you've already found, and the level of details needed. The level of detail increases after you've tried different card combinations that didn't form a set. At level four (last level), the app shows you all four card features of the set in question.

Explore all sets. In a group of of recognized cards, the app finds all sets. You may want to explore all those sets, or know which of the cards form a set.

Color cast? It's essential for the app to recognize all cards correctly. One obstacle might be a color cast in an image caused by certain lighting conditions (e.g., light from blue sky). The app can adapt to those conditions with your help. Tap with your fingertip on your phone's screen, a rectangle will appear (tap must be longer than 0.5 seconds). Drag its center to any card background. The app will mark cards that it recognizes with either a red, green, or purple dot. You may also use this feature to measure color of a colored object in an image, which you may perceive as white (monitor displays red, green, and blue (RGB color) averages of the sample area).

Ready for a challenge? As you gain more experience you may want to challenge yourself, or simply explore different card scenes.

Best Practices

[ You may want to add to each of the items a symbol or image that visualizes the words (fun and probably more memorable). ]
  • Hold your camera parallel to the card surface, otherwise shapes become distorted and therefore hard to recognize.
  • Keep your camera as steady as possible. Sudden movements blur images, which makes it very difficult or impossible to classify card features.
  • Use proper lighting for your scene, because in the dark objects become less distinguishable.
  • If you observe an extreme color cast in your image, use the app's filter that adapts to the light source's color.
  • Please be patient if the app doesn't recognize all cards correctly. A little adjustment, for instance, a slightly different angle between phone and card surface, may yield already to better results.
  • Use the colored marker, which the app draws on each card it recognizes, as first indicator whether or not a card gets seen by the app.
  • Keep in mind that your phone doesn't experience objects as we perceive them by our eye-brain system.

Downloads

As of this writing (November 2019, iOS 12.4.3), Firefox is surprisingly the only browser that allows you to download a video from the web and save it to Photos library on your iPhone. Thus, if you like to have a video on your iPhone from the collection below, we need to ask you to use Firefox. The download and copy procedure is as follows:

  1. On your iPhone, direct Firefox browser window to video collection of card scenes below.
  2. Tap (longer than 0.5 s) on image or download button of card scene you're interested in.
  3. Choose from menu Download Link.
  4. Choose from menu Download Now. By default, Firefox is going to store the video in a folder called Downloads.
  5. Either click immediately on Downloads, or select Settings → Your Library → Downloads →
  6. Select video then choose from menu Save Video. Firefox will add a copy of the video to your Photos library.
  7. Done.

Alternatively, if you have access to an Apple desktop or laptop computer, use your favorite browser and download any video from the list below; after download, open the video on your computer, for instance with QuickTime Player, select File → Share, and share it with your iPhone. Done.

All videos are in MPG-4 format and approximately 2.5 MB in size (duration 0.3 seconds, only a few frames).
