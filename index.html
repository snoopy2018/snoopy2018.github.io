Introducing the Artificial Intelligent Card Player

Practice your rational reasoning and take it to the next level. AI Card Player is a computer vision app which runs on iPhone devices. It helps you learn and master the card game of SET. Card Player analyzes images from camera or file, identifies color, shape, shading, and number of cards, applies and visualizes game rules, and provides hints for finding sets. The app turns your iPhone into a companion of a fascinating and fun card game.

Previews

The following video clips demonstrate Card Player's main features. The animation of yellow disks represent user's fingertip tapping or dragging elements on the screen (for simplicity's sake, swipe gestures and taps that close views are not shown).

Get to know your cards. Tap on any card marked by a colored dot. Card Player will visualize its color, shape, shading, and number.

Set or no set? Select a group of three cards. Card Player will show you whether or not they form a set, and the reason why they do or do not. Observe how the number of sets (top left) found by users increases when they find a new set.

Need a hint? Card Player provides you with hints whenever you need them. A hint is based on sets in a scene, cards you've selected, and sets you've already found. It has four detail levels. At level one, a hint shows either color, shape, shading, or number of three cards that form a set. The level increases after you've tried different card combinations that didn't form a set. At level four, you'll see all four features: color, shape, shading, and number. Or in other words, the full disclosure of a set.

Explore all sets. The app finds all possible sets in a group of recognized cards. If any, tap on the number of sets (top right) and explore them.

Color cast? Card Player needs to recognize all cards precisely. One obstacle might be a color cast caused by lighting conditions (e.g., the blue sky or artificial light). However, the app can adapt to those conditions if you let it know card background color.

Tap with your fingertip on the phone's screen (longer than 0.5 seconds). A rectangle with a sample area in the center will appear. Drag it to any card background. Card Player will analyze its color and then mark recognized cards with either a red, green, or purple dot.

You may use this feature to measure a color composition of any object in an image. The sample monitor displays red, green, and blue (RGB) colors of a sample area (arithmetic means).

Ready for a challenge? As you gain more experience with SET and our app, you may want to challenge yourself, create and explore card scenes, or try to solve problems with help of Card Player. Consider the following. What is the maximum number of sets you can find in a group of N cards? Find a group of 6 cards that consists of 4 sets. Find 20 cards that do not form any set.

Best Practices

The following guidelines helped us during development to achieve high card recognition rates, and we hope they will help you to have the best experience with Card Player.

  • Hold your camera parallel to a card surface, otherwise shapes become distorted and difficult to recognize.
  • Keep your camera as steady as possible. Sudden movements blur images that makes it impossible to classify card features. A rough tap on the Pause button, while using the camera, may also blur the image.
  • Use proper lighting for your scene. Dark objects are less distinguishable.
  • If you observe an extreme color cast, use Card Player's color cast filter which adapts to light source's color.
  • Avoid specular highlights. A game card has a reflective surface, similar to a mirror. If you observe a specular highlight on card images (area without texture), try to move camera until all cards are outside the spotlight. Or, if possible, block direct light to prevent specular reflection and mainly use diffuse reflected light, for example, from a wall or ceiling.
  • Please be patient if Card Player doesn't recognize all cards immediately. Little adjustments, for instance, a slightly different angle between phone and card surface, may already improve recognition rates.
  • Use colored markers, which the app draws on each recognized card, as first indicator whether cards get identified correctly. A marker gets placed in the center of a card. Thus, a marker already indicates two features of a card, color directly and number indirectly.
  • Always keep in mind that your phone doesn't see objects as your eye-brain system lets you perceive them.

Downloads

Feel free to download videos to your iPhone from collection below. A video of a card scene allows you to play at times where you don't have a deck of SET cards at hand.

As of this writing (November 2019, iOS 12.4.3), Firefox is surprisingly the only browser that let you download and save a video on your iPhone. Thus, we need to ask you to use Firefox for this procedure:

  1. On your iPhone, in Firefox navigate to any video from collection below.
  2. Tap on Download Video (longer than 0.5 s).
  3. Select Download Link.
  4. Select Download Now. By default, Firefox will store the video file in a folder called Downloads.
  5. Either tap immediately on Downloads, or navigate to folder: Firefox Settings > Your Library > Downloads.
  6. Tap on the video's filename (e.g., cardScene12Cards6sets.mp4) and select Save Video. Firefox will add a copy to your Photos library.

Alternatively, if you have access to an Apple desktop or laptop computer, use your favorite browser and navigate to video collection below; after downloading a video, open it in your favorite player and share it with your iPhone (in QuickTime Player, select File > Share).

All videos have MPG-4 format and file size of approximately 1 MB (duration about 0.2 seconds).
