Introducing the Artificial Intelligent Card Player

Practice your rational reasoning and take it to the next level. AI Card Player is a computer vision app that assists you to learn and master SET, a card game of visual perception (app runs on an iPhone). It analyses images from camera or file, identifies card features (color, shape, shading, and number), visualizes game rules, and gives you hints when you need them. The app represents an artificial intelligent player or assistant who knows in a group of cards all triplets that form a set.

Either point your phone’s camera at a group of cards or load a recorded card scene, the app’s intuitive user interface and visual concepts make the game and the brain exercise more fun.

You'll find at this webpage Previews that demonstrate the app's core features, Guides that show usage and best practices, and in section Downloads you may download recorded card scenes if you don't have cards at hand.

Previews

The following previews demonstrate core features of the app. The animation of yellow disks represent user's fingertip tapping or dragging elements on screen (taps that close a view are not shown).

Set or no set? A user wants to know whether three cards form a set according to SET rules.

Need a hint? In this scenario the user loads a video, which shows a scene of twelve cards that form six different sets. You may not see any of those sets, or do not find the last one after you've found all the others. In such cases, the app is able to give you a hint which is based on the cards you've selected, the sets in the scene, the sets you've already found, and how many times you've checked different combinations after you've requested your last hint.

Show all sets.The app finds all sets in a group of recognized cards. How does the sets look like?
Color cast? It's essential for the app to recognize all cards correctly. One obstacle might be a color cast in the images caused by certain lighting conditions (e.g., light from the blue sky). The app can adapt to those conditions but needs a little help from you. It needs a color sample from a card background. That's all. This video demonstrates how to do it. In order to initiate this feature you need tap at the phone's screen longer than 0.5 seconds. You can also use this feature to measure and see the color composition, for instance, of the card background under different lighting conditions.

Guides

Demonstration of Use Cases

The following video clips demonstrate common use cases that you may encounter.

  • Load a recorded card scene from your Photos library.
  • Capture a group of cards with the camera and learn features of a card.
  • Select three cards and check whether or not they form a set.
  • Browse through sets found by you or the app.
  • Solve a color cast in an image with a light source color filter.
A tap on a recognized card reveals its features (color, shape, shading, and number).

Best Practices

  • Hold your camera parallel to the card surface, otherwise shapes become distorted and therefore hard to recognize.
  • Keep your camera as steady as possible. Sudden movements blur images, which makes it very difficult or impossible to classify card features.
  • Use proper lighting for your scene, because in the dark objects become less distinguishable.
  • If you observe an extreme color cast in your image, use the app's filter that adapts to the light source's color.

Always keep in mind that your phone doesn’t experience the same image as you perceive by your eye-brain system. Please be patient if the app doesn't recognize all cards correctly. A little adjustment, for instance, a slightly different angle between phone and card surface, may yield already to better results. Use the colored marker, which the app draws on each card it recognizes, as a first indicator whether or not a card gets seen by the app.

Downloads

As of this writing (November 2019), Firefox is surprisingly the only browser that allows to copy a video file from the web to an iPhone. Thus, if you like to have a video on your iPhone from the collection below, we need to ask you to use Firefox. The copy procedure is as follows:

  1. On your iPhone, point the Firefox browser window to the video collection of card scenes below.
  2. Tap on icon (longer than 0.5 s) of card scene you're interested in.
  3. From pop-up menu choose "Download Link".
  4. From pop-up menu choose "Download Now". By default, Firefox is going to store the video in a folder called "Downloads".
  5. Either click in Firefox immediately on "Downloads", or open Files app (part of iOS) and navigate to video.
  6. Select video (in Files tap "Share" icon after you've selected video) then choose from menu "Save Video". Both will add a copy of the video to your Photos library.
  7. Done.
